Understanding the problem

COVID-19 has a significant impact on the physical and mental health of elderly people. Social distancing measures and lockdowns have made it challenging for them to stay active and engaged, which increased social isolation and a higher risk of depression and anxiety. Moreover, some old people have a higher risk of severe illness from COVID-19, which can worsen pre-existing health issues and limit their ability to participate in physical activities and social interactions. Therefore, this app aims to provide a solution to these challenges by offering the following features:

  • Exercises that can be done at home to support the elderlyâ€™s physical abilities.
  • Access to mental health resources and support networks.
  • Reminders, such as taking medications and keeping up with doctor's appointments.
  • Resources about COVID-19. For example, the latest news, prevention tips, and what to do when having the symptoms.
  • Stress-free News accessibility.
  • Connection: Chat with other elderly that may have common interests.

Surveys

Methodology of the survey :

To ensure that our app meets the needs of the elderly users in the Greater Montreal area, we conducted a thoughtful survey at several nursing homes. In this survey, we respectfully approached residents aged 60 and over to ask about their mental well-being and what type of features they would appreciate in a mental health app. In recognition of the fact that some elderly users may not be as familiar with technology, we asked questions that were carefully designed to grasp their wants and needs. We are optimistic that the results of our survey will guide us in developing an app that truly addresses the unique requirements of our target audience.



The results of the survey were then put into charts for better visualization of the data gathered.




After conducting a thorough survey, we discovered that the majority of elderly individuals use mobile phones on a daily basis. In response, we created a mobile application that is easily accessible and user-friendly. We tailored the app's features based on the survey results, such as the 90% of participants who expressed the need for a reminder feature, and 66.7% who requested the ability to engage in physical exercises and chat with friends. Additionally, the survey revealed that participants preferred simple and intuitive menus and navigation buttons, and desired quick access to the app's features. These valuable insights from the survey helped us plan and develop the app's design, ensuring that it meets the needs and preferences of our target audience.

Visual Design

Color Palette

We carefully selected a pastel color palette for our website design, taking into consideration the unique needs of our elderly users. Pastel colors are known for their softness and soothing effect on the eyes, making them a perfect fit for our user demographic. We wanted to create a welcoming and comfortable online environment, and using these gentle hues helps to achieve that goal. Additionally, pastel colors have a timeless quality that can lend a sense of stability and reliability to our website, further enhancing the user experience.


Typography

We chose the font Roboto because it is a clean, modern, and easy-to-read font that enhances the readability and legibility of the website content, especially for elderly users who may have vision impairments. Additionally, Roboto has a simple and straightforward design that complements the overall clean and minimalist design of the website, creating a cohesive and professional look.

Final Design

The final design of the app features a simple and user-friendly interface with easy navigation and visually appealing graphics. The home page consists of six buttons, providing quick access to essential features such as Covid-19 updates, mental well-being, reminders, exercise, chat, and positive news. The Covid-19 updates page offers reliable information from trusted sources, while the mental well-being page provides various breathing exercises, tips, and audio feedback for users struggling with different mental health issues. The reminders page allows users to set reminders for their medications and important dates, ensuring they never miss an appointment or medication dose. The exercise page offers a wide range of exercises, along with instructions and equipment needed, to help seniors stay physically active. Finally, the chat page provides a platform for users to connect with like-minded people, adding them using their email or social media. Overall, the final design of the app caters to the unique needs of elderly people and aims to improve their physical and mental health and overall well-being.

Setting Up The Account

Our setup page is designed to be user-friendly and simple, guiding users through the process step by step. We ask for all necessary information in a clear and straightforward manner, using visual elements to make it easy for elderly users to understand and input their information.

Pages

Main page     

The main page is designed to be simple and easy to navigate, with only six buttons that lead directly to the information or features needed. This ensures a straightforward user experience, allowing elderly users to quickly access the tools they need to support their mental and physical health.


COVID-19 News     

The COVID updates page is a simple and effective tool for keeping elderly users informed and up-to-date on the latest news and information related to COVID-19. The page features three easy-to-use buttons: Symptoms, Prevention Tips, and Updates, that lead users to reliable and trusted sources of information, such as government websites and health organizations. The design is simple and straightforward, ensuring that users can access the information they need quickly and easily. By providing access to reliable and up-to-date information, the COVID updates page helps users stay informed and take steps to protect their health during these challenging times.


Mental Wellbeing     

The mental well-being page is designed to cater to various mental health struggles such as stress, sleep, loneliness, and focus. Users can select their preferred option and access breathing exercises, tips, and audio feedback to help with their struggles. Additionally, there is a button to connect users to a list of certified therapists for further assistance.


News     

Our "Positive News" page is a source of uplifting and inspiring news, away from the negativity and stress in the world. It features heartwarming stories, motivational quotes, and positive events from around the globe, helping to promote mental wellbeing and positivity in our users.


Reminders     

Our "Reminders" page is a user-friendly feature of our app that helps elderly people manage their medication and important dates. With a simple interface, users can easily set reminders for their medications, doctor appointments, and special occasions. The app allows users to enter medication details and set specific dates and times for events. There is also an option to snooze or dismiss reminders. Our goal is to reduce stress and anxiety associated with medication management and promote the overall well-being of elderly people.


Exercise     

Our "Exercise" page is a valuable feature of our mobile application designed to support the physical health of elderly people. This page provides an easy-to-use interface that allows users to select exercises based on their fitness level and equipment available. Users can access detailed instructions and images to perform exercises correctly, ensuring that they receive the maximum benefit. Additionally, the app suggests exercises that require minimal equipment or can be performed at home, making it accessible to all users. With this feature, our app aims to promote physical activity and help seniors maintain their health and mobility.

Conclusion

Based on the insights gathered from our survey and research, we believe that the development of a mobile application to support the mental and physical health of elderly people is a critical initiative. The app should incorporate features that are tailored to meet the unique needs of this demographic, such as mental health assistance, reminders, positive news and social connectivity . The chooses we made for the app design can create a comfortable and welcoming experience for the elderly. By addressing the challenges faced by seniors during the pandemic and beyond, this app has the potential to improve the quality of life of millions of elderly individuals worldwide. We look forward to the implementation of this initiative and the positive impact it can have on the elderly community.
